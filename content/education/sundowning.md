Sundowning refers to increased confusion, agitation, or anxiety that appears in the late afternoon or evening. It is common in mid-to-late stage dementia and can involve pacing, mood swings, or becoming suspicious of caregivers.

## Why it happens

The exact cause isn't fully understood, but it may be related to disruptions in the internal body clock, fatigue accumulating through the day, or reduced light triggering confusion about time.

## What helps

- **Routine:** A consistent daily schedule reduces uncertainty. Try to eat, bathe, and do activities at the same time every day.
- **Light:** Bright light exposure in the morning and reduced light in the afternoon can help regulate the body clock.
- **Activity:** A gentle walk or light activity in the late morning can reduce restlessness later.
- **Environment:** Keep the home calm and well-lit (but not harshly bright) in the early evening.

## When to ask for help

If sundowning behaviors are becoming dangerous (wandering outside, aggressive outbursts) or are significantly disrupting sleep, speak with the care recipient's doctor about additional strategies.
