Repetitive questions are one of the most common and emotionally draining aspects of caring for someone with dementia. The person asks the same question minutes apart — not to frustrate you, but because they cannot retain the answer.

## Why it happens

Short-term memory loss means each question feels new to the person asking it. The emotion behind the question (anxiety, boredom, a need for reassurance) often persists even when the memory of asking does not.

## What helps

- **Don't correct or remind them they already asked.** This causes shame and rarely helps.
- **Answer the emotion, not just the fact.** If "When is dinner?" is asked repeatedly, they may be hungry or anxious — try offering a snack or reassurance.
- **Written reminders:** A whiteboard with key facts ("Dinner is at 6pm. Today is Tuesday.") lets them check without needing to ask.
- **Redirection:** After answering, gently guide attention to something enjoyable — music, a photo album, a snack.

## Taking care of yourself

Repetitive questions can cause significant caregiver stress. It helps to remind yourself that the behavior is a symptom of the disease, not a choice. If you feel yourself becoming irritated, step away for a few minutes if it is safe to do so.
