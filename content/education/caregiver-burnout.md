Caregiver burnout is a state of physical, emotional, and mental exhaustion that can occur when you give more than you are able to give. It is not a sign of weakness — it is a sign that the demands of caregiving have exceeded your current support.

## Warning signs

- Withdrawing from friends, family, or activities you used to enjoy
- Feeling resentful or hopeless about caregiving
- Neglecting your own health (skipping meals, doctor appointments, sleep)
- Snapping at the person you care for or at others
- Feeling like nothing you do makes a difference

## What helps

- **Respite:** Regular time away from caregiving — even a few hours — is essential, not optional.
- **Delegation:** Use Carelog to assign specific tasks to other family members so the burden is shared.
- **Professional support:** A therapist or counselor familiar with caregiver stress can be valuable.
- **Physical health:** Prioritize sleep, movement, and medical care for yourself. You cannot pour from an empty cup.

## Remember

Recognizing burnout early makes it much easier to address. If you are noticing these signs, tell someone on your care team today.
